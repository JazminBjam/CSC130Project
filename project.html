Environmental Exploration


Environmental Exploration Vision Statement:

"Inspiring people to explore their physical environment through the help of extended reality. Our goal is to provide the suitable applications needed to expand accessibility for people with disabilities and mobility struggles while allowing the same access to beautiful hidden landscapes in their area."



About



2D: Our website shows a map of different activities based on the physical environment. The map shows a general overview of each area based on your location.


3D: Once hitting a specific pinpoint for a location, users can see 3d images of the locations and explore before actually going.



Using extended reality within the physical environment can lead to greater accessibility for exploring the natural world.



People spend a lot of wasted time looking for nice hiking spots, hidden beachs, and artifacts. Having this application would aid in time efficiency and planning, as well as having a more inclusive accessibility needs, such as wheelchair accessibility, elderly, or mobility issues.



Focusing on the lack of accessibility for people wanting to explore the physical environment, as well as focusing on using the 3D feature at home for an individual to access their own needs before exploring the specific location.



  • Pro: Able to use at home on a mobile phone
  • Pro: Easier to visualize
  • Con: Need a base knowledge for technology, additional technology programs, and how to use it which could be harder for elderly


  • Pro: 3D allows for a better understanding of the area compared to photos
  • Con: Harder to code and program
  • Con: Need resources and be able to go to the location before to create in 3D


  • AR: A general basis on what the locations look like in 3D, able to use on phone.
  • MR: More in depth and able to interact for a deeper understanding of location.
  • VR: If unable to visit the physical environment location, can still visit through virtual reality.


Similar idea: Findery - A free app for Iphone and Android available worldwide, lets people view trivia and stories about favorite locations, whether it’s a building, beach, or a landmark like the Eiffel Tower.
